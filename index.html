Identity & Access for Cardholders

How users verify identity, authorize access and activate cards in modern card programs.

What is “access” in card systems?

Access involves how a user proves who they are, activates a card, links devices, unlocks features, and securely authenticates transactions.

  • KYC identity verification
  • Strong Customer Authentication (SCA)
  • In-app card activation flows
  • PIN & device binding
  • Biometrics (face / fingerprint)
  • On-device tokens and secure elements

Core access flows

1. Identity verification (KYC)

ID document scan, selfie match and address checks before a card can be issued.

2. Device binding

The user’s phone is turned into an authentication device, storing keys or tokens.

3. Card activation

The issuer proves you control the card before enabling online and tap payments.

4. Authentication

PIN, biometrics or app confirmation for login, payments and high-risk actions.

How authentication works

Method Used for
PIN Physical transactions & fallback verification.
Biometrics App login, card controls and SCA challenges.
Push approval High-risk actions: large payments and password resets.
3DS challenge Online purchases requiring verified identity.
Token binding Ensures a card token only works on one device.

Why secure access matters

  • Prevents identity fraud
  • Stops account takeover
  • Makes lost/stolen card scenarios safer
  • Required for PSD2 and global SCA rules
  • Enables higher limits and advanced features
